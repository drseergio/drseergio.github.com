News sources

Nov 26th, 2013

I find it difficult to find quality sources of news, thought provoking essays and articles. A good selection of sources is a balancing act. If the selection is small you don’t get to stimulate thinking and expand knowledge. Get it too big and you’re overwhelmed. Presented below is a curated list of blogs and websites that I subscribe to.

I hope you discover something new. I know my search is not over. I’d like to know what are your most valued sources.

Last updated on: 2013-12-25.

Fueling curiosity

  • Farnam Street – publishes excerpts from books and interesting ideas. If I had to choose just one blog/site this would be it.

  • Nautilus – science magazine with a wide coverage of topics.

  • Raptitude – witty and illuminating essays on various topics in life.

  • Aeon magazine – Aeon Magazine is an online magazine about nature, culture and ideas.

  • Aeon film – Aeon Film screens contemporary and classic short documentaries, exploring the intersections between nature, culture and ideas.

  • Dan Ariely – blog of a well known behavioral economics researcher and the author of “Predictably irrational”.

  • Derek Sivers – blog of the creator of CD baby.

  • Successful Software – blog of a software developer running a successful one man company.

Photography

  • Digital Photography School – regular tips and guides on digital photography. I like the amount of published material. Frequent enough to be interesting to follow but not so much to become overwhelming.

Early retirement/financial independence

  • Early retirement extreme – a combination of simple living, anticonsumerism, DIY ethics, self-reliance, and applied capitalism.

  • Mr Money Moustache – achieving financial independence early through smart decisions and better life decisions.

  • Financial Samurai – personal finance blog focusing on financial independence.

Personal development

  • Steve Pavlina – I don’t believe in everything Steve writes but more often than not his articles provoke deep introspective thoughts and fuel an impetus for change.

Technology

  • Hacker News 150 – a filtered list of items from Hacker News – social news aggregator popular with hackers, software engineers and entrepreneurs. Even with filtering I read less than 1/3 of all items.

  • Schneier on Security – blog of a famous security technologist.

  • I, Cringely – blog of a famous technology writer.

  • Coding Horror – blog from the creator of stackoverflow.com.

Fun

  • xkcd comic – no introduction necessary.

  • oatmeal comic – no introduction necessary.

Purged/deprecated

At some point I have been subscribed to the feeds below but I no longer follow them:

  • Habrahabr – (in Russian) an online tech-oriented community/aggregator. Most of the articles are written from scratch, rather than republished. I had to spent considerable amount of time unsubscribing from different channels to bring volume down to a manageable level. But apart from that inconvenience there is lots of interesting material that is not published in any other source that I read. Too much material to subscribe to. Might return to it if I find a narrow niche.
